To make the output, you have to... copy the input grid. Then, you will add 4 2x2 blocks to the grid, each touching the corner of the 2x2 colored square in the input. If the 2x2 colored square is not in the center of the input, some of the 2x2 blocks you are adding may be off the page.

Option 1

To make the output, you have to... erase the input grid. Then, you will remove 4 2x2 blocks from the grid, each touching the corner of the 2x2 colored square in the input. The color of each 2x2 block should be completely different from the opposite corner of the 2x2 colored square. If the 2x2 colored square is in the center of the input, none of the 2x2 blocks you are removing will be off the page.

Option 2

If the 2x2 colored square is horizontally centered, the 2x2 blocks you are placing may overlap with each other.

Option 3

The color of each 2x2 block should be a mixture of all colors of the 2x2 colored square. If the 2x2 colored square is at the corner of the input, the 2x2 blocks you are rotating will be at the middle of the page.

Option 4

To make the output, you have to... cut out the input grid. Then, you will position 4 2x2 blocks under the grid, each aligning with the corner of the 2x2 colored square in the input. The color of each 2x2 block should be the darkest shade of the 2x2 colored square. If the 2x2 colored square is along the border of the input, the 2x2 blocks you are positioning will be covered by the page.
